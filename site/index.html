Beat 02 — What it is

Above its trend, hold BTC. Below it, go to cash.

One line — BTC's moving average — splits the whole strategy. Price above it: hold BTC. Price below it: hold stablecoins. That's the entire rule.

It never nails the exact top or bottom — it just stays in for the big uptrends and out for the deep crashes. Over years, that's what compounds.

Under the hood

A small, deterministic state machine.

01

Read. BTC spot price and its moving average from CMC — get_crypto_quotes_latest and get_crypto_technical_analysis — plus Fear & Greed from get_global_metrics_latest for context.

02

Decide. price > MA → RISK-ON: hold BTC. price < MA → RISK-OFF: move to stablecoins. No prediction, no thresholds to tune mid-trade — just which side of the line price is on.

03

Report. Current regime, the position to hold, and the single number that flips it — the exact price level of the moving average. Auditable and rule-bound.

One parameter — the MA window — sets the tempo: 50-day is more active and aggressive, 200-day calmer. Default is 100-day. Everything else is fixed, so the strategy is fully deterministic and backtestable.

From signal to position

How you'd actually run it.

The skill decides; you execute — it never touches your funds. The whole loop:

01

Ask the Skill. "Is BTC above its trend?" It returns RISK-ON → hold BTC or RISK-OFF → hold cash, plus the exact moving-average price that flips it.

02

If RISK-ON → hold BTC. Buy or keep BTC (BTCB) spot on PancakeSwap with a wallet like Trust Wallet on BNB Chain. Then just hold while the trend is up.

03

If RISK-OFF → hold stablecoins. Sell BTC to USDT/USDC and wait out the downtrend. No shorting, no leverage — just out of the market.

04

Re-check daily. The signal only changes on a trend flip — a handful of times a year. When it flips, you rotate BTC ↔ stablecoins once. That's the entire job.

Non-custodial by design. The Skill never holds your keys, never moves your money, and never auto-trades. It produces an auditable signal — every trade is made by you, on venues you control.

Not financial advice — you bear your own risk

This project is an educational research tool built for a hackathon. Nothing here is financial, investment, or trading advice, and nothing is a recommendation to buy, sell, or hold any asset. You are solely responsible for your own decisions, your own funds, and your own risk.

Trend-following is not risk-free. It whipsaws in choppy, directionless markets — shaken out low, bought back high, bleeding small losses in a row. It always lags the exact top and bottom by design. The backtest uses daily Binance data with transaction costs applied, but live slippage, gas, and execution will differ. Crucially, 2023–2026 was largely a bull market, which flatters any long/trend strategy — the headline returns shown will not repeat.

Out-of-sample testing reduces but does not eliminate overfitting risk. We backtested 27 strategies, and the more you test, the more a lucky one can slip through. We guard against it by requiring a whole family to hold up on data it never saw — but no backtest guarantees the future. Past performance does not predict future results.

Do your own research. Only deploy capital you can afford to lose.
