Collect the funding rate. Not the price.

A CoinMarketCap Agent Skill that runs a delta-neutral BTC carry — long spot, short perp — to collect the perpetual funding rate. It reads live CMC data and decides when funding is worth collecting, and when to sit in stablecoins.

Beat 02 — What it is

You buy spot. You short the perp. Price risk cancels out.

Hold $10k of BTC spot, short $10k of BTC on a perpetual. If BTC moves, the two legs cancel — your price P&L is ~zero. What's left is the funding rate: the fee longs pay shorts on perps, settled every 8 hours. When it's positive, you (the short) collect it.

+1 BTC long

Net price exposure
Delta ≈ 0 → return = funding

On BTC, funding has been positive ~86% of the time since 2023, averaging ~7% annualized. That's the yield this captures — paid for taking the other side of leverage demand, not for predicting price.

Beat 04 — The insight, proven

Turnover kills the carry. We discovered it in the data.

Versions that chase funding — entering and exiting as it wobbles around zero — all lose money. Two legs, fees each way, hundreds of trades: the friction eats the yield. The version that wins holds the position and only exits on a deep-negative circuit breaker. 2 trades in 3.4 years.

This is not a Bitcoin-beater. It's a market-neutral position: ~5% a year unlevered, about the same as stablecoin lending — but with no price exposure. Because there's no price risk you can lever it: 5× returns ~16% a year at a −4.5% max drawdown. Judge it on Sharpe and drawdown, not raw return. Leverage adds liquidation risk the backtest can't model — see the disclaimer.

Under the hood

A small, deterministic state machine.

01

Read the signal. Market-wide funding, open interest and liquidations from get_global_crypto_derivatives_metrics; Fear & Greed from get_global_metrics_latest; BTC spot price.

02

Decide. FLAT → ENTER when annualized funding ≥ +5%. DEPLOYED → EXIT only when funding falls below the −15% circuit breaker. Otherwise HOLD — the most common, correct action.

03

Report. A concrete, auditable allocation: deploy %, both legs, and the single trigger that would change the position.

CMC only exposes aggregate funding, so the skill uses it as a regime signal and runs the carry on the single deepest-liquidity leg (BTC). Simpler, more robust, fully CMC-native.

From signal to position

How you'd actually run the carry.

The skill decides; you execute. It reads the market and outputs the action — it never touches your funds. The full loop:

01

Ask the Skill. "Should I be in a funding carry right now?" It returns a clear call — ENTER, HOLD, or EXIT — with a deploy size and the single trigger that would change it.

02

If it says HOLD or EXIT → do nothing. Stay in stablecoins. Most of the time this is the correct move, and it costs zero fees. Patience is the strategy.

03

If it says ENTER → fund a BSC wallet. Move stablecoins (USDT / USDC) into a wallet like Trust Wallet on BNB Chain.

04

Open the long leg. Buy BTC (BTCB) spot on PancakeSwap for the deploy amount.

05

Open the short leg. Short the same notional in the BTC perpetual on ApolloX, keeping a ~30% stablecoin margin buffer so the short can't get liquidated on a price spike. Now your delta is ≈ 0.

06

Collect & re-check. Funding accrues every 8 hours. Re-ask the Skill on a schedule; when it flips to EXIT (funding below the −15% circuit breaker), close both legs and return to stablecoins.

Non-custodial by design. The Skill never holds your keys, never moves your money, and never auto-trades. It produces an auditable recommendation — every position is opened by you, on venues you control.

Not financial advice — you bear your own risk

This project is an educational research tool built for a hackathon. Nothing here is financial, investment, or trading advice, and nothing is a recommendation to buy, sell, or hold any asset. You are solely responsible for your own decisions, your own funds, and your own risk.

A delta-neutral funding carry is not risk-free. Real risks include short-leg liquidation, funding turning negative, basis drift between spot and perp, stablecoin de-peg, smart-contract and venue failure, and slippage. The backtest assumes a perfect hedge and historical Binance data as a proxy for on-chain venues; live results will differ and can be negative. Past performance does not predict future results.

Leverage multiplies both gains and losses and adds real liquidation risk that this idealized, perfect-hedge backtest does not capture. The 2× and 5× curves shown are far riskier than their smooth lines suggest — a sharp adverse move can wipe the margin and close the position at a loss. Higher leverage is not "more free yield."

Do your own research. Only deploy capital you can afford to lose.
