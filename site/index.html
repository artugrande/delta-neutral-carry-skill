Beat 02 — What it is

Above its trend, hold BTC. Below it, go to cash.

One line — BTC's moving average — splits the whole strategy. Price above it: hold BTC. Price below it: hold stablecoins. That's the entire rule.

Price > its average
Hold BTC
Price < its average
Move to cash
Result, 2023–2026 (100-day)
More upside, a third of the drawdown

It never nails the exact top or bottom — it just stays in for the big uptrends and out for the deep crashes. Over years, that's what compounds.

What about the future?

We won't promise a number. Here's the honest version.

The curve looks steeper after 2025 — but that's compounding, not the strategy getting smarter. A +40% year on a bigger base just adds more dollars; on a log axis the slope is steady. A good past doesn't buy a good future.

What should persist is structural: as long as crypto keeps having big trends and big crashes, the rule keeps you in for the rallies and out for the −50% drops. That drawdown control is the durable edge. The return depends on what BTC does next — which nobody can promise.

Under the hood

A small, deterministic state machine.

01

Read. BTC price and its moving average from CMC (get_crypto_quotes_latest, get_crypto_technical_analysis); Fear & Greed for context.

02

Decide. price > MA → RISK-ON, hold BTC. price < MA → RISK-OFF, hold stablecoins. Just which side of the line price is on.

03

Report. The regime, the position, and the one number that flips it — the MA price level. Auditable, rule-bound.

One knob — the MA window — sets the tempo (50-day aggressive, 200-day calm; 100-day default). Everything else is fixed: fully deterministic, fully backtestable.

From signal to position

How you'd actually run it.

The skill decides; you execute — it never touches your funds. The whole loop:

01

Ask the Skill. "Is BTC above its trend?" It returns RISK-ON → hold BTC or RISK-OFF → hold cash, plus the exact moving-average price that flips it.

02

If RISK-ON → hold BTC. Buy or keep BTC (BTCB) spot on PancakeSwap (e.g. via Trust Wallet on BNB Chain). Hold while the trend is up.

03

If RISK-OFF → hold stablecoins. Sell BTC to USDT/USDC and wait out the downtrend. No shorting, no leverage — just out of the market.

04

Re-check daily. The signal flips only a handful of times a year; when it does, you rotate BTC ↔ stablecoins once. That's the whole job.

Non-custodial by design. The skill never holds your keys, moves your money, or auto-trades. It outputs an auditable signal — every trade is yours, on venues you control.

Hands-off

Or: let an agent run it for you.

The manual loop works — but the whole thing fits in one scheduled function on Vercel. Nobody triggers a trade by hand.

01

Vercel Cron fires once a day and calls a serverless function — the only "trigger" in the whole system.

02

The function pulls BTC price + its moving average from the CoinMarketCap MCP, through the Vercel AI SDK's MCP client.

03

The rule runs in code, not in the model: price > MA ? hold BTC : stablecoins. The LLM can narrate; it never decides the trade — so the agent stays reproducible.

04

On a regime flip only, it sends a Telegram alert and (optionally) executes the swap. No flip → it does nothing.

// vercel.json — the only scheduler
"crons": [{ "path": "/api/trend-tick", "schedule": "0 12 * * *" }]

// api/trend-tick.js — the decision is plain code, not the LLM
const target = price > movingAverage ? "BTC" : "STABLE";
if (target !== lastRegime) notifyAndExecute(target);

Built on Vercel's agent stack — Cron, Functions, the AI SDK MCP client, and the AI Gateway. For the demo it runs notify-only (paper trade) — live wallet execution is the same code path, opt-in once keys and risk limits are in place.

The engine

Built to run on Claude Opus 4.8.

Why this model for an always-on agent that touches money? Three things matter more than raw IQ:

01

Reliable tool use. Opus 4.8 specifically improved tool triggering — it doesn't skip the price, balance, or execute calls a trading tick depends on.

02

Long-horizon stability. A 1M-token context and steadier long agent loops mean it doesn't derail over a run that lives for months.

03

Honest about failure. It glosses over hidden failures only ~3.7% of the time (per Anthropic's Opus 4.8 announcement) — a money agent has to surface bad data and failed fills, not bury them.

And it's deterministic by design: structured JSON outputs, behavior driven by explicit rules rather than random sampling. The model orchestrates and explains; the strategy stays a hard rule. More on Claude Opus 4.8 →

Not financial advice — you bear your own risk

This project is an educational research tool built for a hackathon. Nothing here is financial, investment, or trading advice, and nothing is a recommendation to buy, sell, or hold any asset. You are solely responsible for your own decisions, your own funds, and your own risk.

Trend-following is not risk-free. It whipsaws in choppy markets — shaken out low, bought back high, bleeding small losses. It always lags the exact top and bottom. The backtest uses daily Binance data with costs applied; live slippage, gas, and timing differ. And 2023–2026 was largely a bull market, which flatters any long/trend strategy — the headline returns will not repeat.

Out-of-sample testing reduces overfitting risk but doesn't remove it: we tested 27 strategies, and the more you test, the easier a lucky one slips through. We guard against it by requiring a whole family to hold up on unseen data — but no backtest guarantees the future. Past performance does not predict future results.

Do your own research. Only deploy capital you can afford to lose.
